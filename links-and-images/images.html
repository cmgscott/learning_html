Images

This page covers common image formats, but you may also be looking for links and useful extras.

JPGs

JPG images are good for photos and images with lots of gradients in them because they are designed for handling large color palettes without increasing file size too much. CON: they don't allow for transparent pixels

GIFs

GIFs are somewhat limited in terms of color palette, and transparency is binary (off/on).

PNGs

PNGs are good for everything that's not a photo or animated, and they deal with opacity fine and don't have color palette limitations. Good for icons, tech diagrams, logos, etc.

SVGs

SVG is vector-based format, scales up or down to any dimension without loss of quality. CON: must convert all text fields to outlines using image editor. Good for responsive design, same uses as PNGs (use them whenever you can).
